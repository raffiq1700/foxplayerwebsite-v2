The excitement of starting your first algo can often lead to "Blind Optimism." You build a strategy that looks perfect on paper, but it fails the moment it hits the live market. 

At **Foxplayer Algo Technologies**, we’ve seen it all. Here are the 10 most common mistakes that kill a beginner's trading career before it even starts.

---

## 1. Over-Optimizing (Curve Fitting)
Beginners often tweak their strategy rules until it shows a 99% win rate on historical data. This is called **Curve Fitting**. In the real world, the future never looks exactly like the past. 
*   **The Fix:** Keep your logic simple. A strategy with 3 simple rules is always more robust than one with 15 complex rules.

## 2. Ignoring Slippage and Taxes
Your backtest might say you made ₹5,000. But after you account for brokerage, STT (Securities Transaction Tax), and the difference in execution price (Slippage), you might actually be in a loss.
*   **The Fix:** Always subtract 0.5% to 1% from your backtest results to get a "Real World" estimate.

## 3. Trading Too Big (Over-Leveraging)
Just because your broker gives you 5x leverage doesn't mean you should use it. One "Flash Crash" can wipe out your entire account if you are over-leveraged.
*   **The Fix:** Start with a position size where a single loss only affects 1-2% of your capital.

## 4. Interference (The "Hands-on" Mistake)
The biggest mistake is pausing your algo just because you "feel" the market is going to crash. If you interfere, you break the statistical probability of the system.
*   **The Fix:** Trust your backtest. If the system says "Buy," let it buy.

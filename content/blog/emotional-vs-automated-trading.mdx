Why is it so easy to buy a stock that's rising, but so incredibly painful to sell it when it's falling?

The answer lies in **Neuroscience**. 

When we face financial loss, our brain's amygdala—the part responsible for the "fight or flight" response—fires up. It treats a 2% drop in your portfolio the same way it treats a predator attacking you. You stop thinking logically and start acting for survival.

---

## 1. The Anatomy of a Trade

### The Emotional Cycle
1.  **Optimism:** "This strategy looks great!"
2.  **Thrill:** The trade is up ₹2,000.
3.  **Euphoria:** "I'm a genius! I should buy more."
4.  **Anxiety:** The market dips. "It's just a correction."
5.  **Panic:** It keeps falling. "I need to get out now!"

By the time you hit "Panic," the damage is already done. You sold at the bottom.

## 3. The Power of "Set and Forget"

The greatest advantage of using an algo is the ability to walk away. When you are not staring at the "M2M" (Mark-to-Market) profit and loss fluctuating every second, you can't make emotional mistakes.

You allow the **Law of Large Numbers** to work in your favor. If your strategy has a 60% win rate, you simply need to execute 100 trades to see the profit. An emotional human will quit after 3 losses. An algo will keep going until the 100th trade.

## 1. How the Bridge Works
The technology that connects TradingView to Foxplayer is called a **Webhook**. A Webhook is a way for one website to send a "signal" to another website instantly.

**The Workflow:**
1.  Your indicator on TradingView hits a "Buy" condition.
2.  TradingView sends a "Webhook" signal to Foxplayer's servers.
3.  Foxplayer receives the signal, verifies your account, and instantly places the trade with your broker (AliceBlue, Zerodha, etc.).

## 2. Step-by-Step Setup

### Step A: Prepare Your Strategy on TradingView
Ensure your indicator or strategy is working exactly how you want it. Set up an alert on the indicator.

### Step B: Get Your Foxplayer Webhook URL
Log in to your Foxplayer dashboard and go to the "TradingView" section. You will see a unique URL specifically for your account. Copy it.

### Step C: Configure the Alert
In the TradingView Alert settings:
1.  Go to the "Notifications" tab.
2.  Check the **Webhook URL** box.
3.  Paste your Foxplayer URL.
4.  In the "Message" box, paste the JSON code provided by Foxplayer (this tells us which stock and how many lots to buy).

## 3. Why Use Webhooks?
*   **Zero Coding:** You don't need to know Python or C++. If you can copy and paste, you can automate.
*   **Cloud Reliability:** Your computer doesn't need to be on. Once the alert is set, TradingView's servers talk to Foxplayer's servers directly.
*   **Versatility:** You can automate anything—from a simple Moving Average crossover to a complex multi-timeframe strategy.

## 4. Common Mistakes to Avoid
1.  **Alert Frequency:** Ensure your alert is set to "Once Per Bar Close" to avoid "repainting" or multiple accidental trades.
2.  **Internet Latency:** While Webhooks are fast, they depend on the TradingView servers. Using a professional bridge like Foxplayer ensures the lowest possible delay.
